Prince Charming




Prince Charming is a stock character who appears in a number of fairy tales. He is the prince who comes to the rescue

 of the damsel in distress, and stereotypically, must engage in a quest to liberate her from an evil spell. This classification

 suits most heroes of a number of traditional folk tales, including Snow White, Sleeping Beauty and Cinderella, even if in the 

original story they were given another name, or no name at all.

These characters are often handsome and romantic, a foil to the heroine, and are seldom deeply characterized, 

or even distinguishable from other such men who marry the heroine. In many variants, they can be viewed more as rewards 

for the heroine rather than characters.[1] "Prince Charming" is also used as a term to refer to the idealized man some people 

dream of as a future spouse.[2] Mail Online referred to Chris O'Neill, a New York financier whom Princess Madeleine of Sweden 

fell in love with, as "Millionaire Prince Charming".[3]
