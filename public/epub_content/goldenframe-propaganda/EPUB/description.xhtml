During the period of World Wars propaganda has been one of the main tools for manipulating public opinion. The content mainly covered militaristic activities, rasist and (anti)religious ideologies, education of the youth, freedom of speech and the ultimate power of totalitarian leaders.

 

Especially in Bolschevik Russia, propoganda had developed into a form of art, videly popular among local painters (notably Dadaists). The ideas then spread into Western Europe to serve local regimes. Commonly shared design concepts were predefined propoganda symbolism as well as similar (red, black, white) color schemes. Even if represented ideas were mainly designed to serve as weapons between Eastern and Western blocks, they shared same visual means of expressing ideologies.

 

In various parts of the world, also mainly due to international propaganda as well as local circumstances (Fascist repressions in France and Italy, Soviet occupations in Eastern Europe) the Super States and their regimes were regarded differently.
