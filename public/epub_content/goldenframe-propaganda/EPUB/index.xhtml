Golden Frame Propaganda

 

Vytautas Jankauskas

HEAD Geneva

Open-Source Publishing Workshop

 

 

 

During the period of World Wars propaganda has been one of the main tools of controlled

information and the manipulation of public opinion. The content mainly covered militaristic

activities, rasist and atheist or cultist ideologies, education of the youth, freedom of speech and

the ultimate power of totalitarian leaders.

 

Subliminal messages often targeted enemy countries and alliaces. Principal concepts

covered the differences (or more particularly, inferiority) between various forms of goverment,

economy, state religion,nationalism, etc.

 

Especially in Bolschevik Russia, propoganda had developed into an independent form of art,

videly popular among local painters (mostly Dadaists). The ideas then spread into Western

Europe to serve local regimes. The interesting commonly shared design concept was popular

propoganda symbolism as well as similar color schemes. Even if the represented ideas

were mainly designed to serve as weapons between Eastern and Western blocks, they

interestingly shared same means of expressing ideologies.

 

Another interesting factor is that in different parts of world, also mainly due to international

propaganda and also local circumstances (Fascist repressions in France and Italy, Soviet

occupations in Eastern Europe) the Super States and their regimes were regarded differently.

In other words, the USSR which was regarded as Europeâ€™s saviour and the winner of the

World War 2, is still considered as one of the biggest repressors in the East, and to this date

the tention between both sides remains.

 

Golden Frame Propaganda is a unpretentious conceptual reflection of how similar the

ideologies and ways of mass control were independently of the differences between regimes.
